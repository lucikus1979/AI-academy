## What to Create

A complete agent definition with three components:

### Component 1: System Prompt (max 500 words)
Instructions that define your agent's behavior, capabilities, and constraints.

### Component 2: Three Examples
Input → Expected Output pairs showing how the agent works.

### Component 3: Boundaries Definition
What the agent does NOT do (equally important as what it does).

## Evaluation Criteria

| Criterion | Weight | What We Look For |
|-----------|--------|------------------|
| **Single Purpose** | 25% | Agent has ONE clear, specific purpose |
| **Clear Boundaries** | 25% | Explicit about what it does NOT do |
| **Quality Examples** | 25% | Examples are realistic and demonstrate range |
| **Practical Value** | 25% | Would actually be useful in real work |

### What Earns Full Points

✅ Specific, narrow scope (not "handles everything")
✅ Thoughtful constraints and limitations
✅ Examples that show normal AND edge cases
✅ Clear connection to real work scenario
✅ Professional, well-structured document

### What Loses Points

❌ Too vague or too broad scope
❌ No boundaries defined
❌ Only happy-path examples
❌ Copy-pasted from somewhere
❌ No clear business value

## Tips for Success

### DO:
- **Start with YOUR work** - What do you do repeatedly?
- **Be specific** - "Summarizes meeting notes" > "Helps with meetings"
- **Think failures** - What should agent refuse? When should it escalate?
- **Test mentally** - Walk through your examples, do they make sense?
- **Use AI Tutor** - Discuss your idea, get feedback before submitting

### DON'T:
- **Don't be generic** - "General assistant" is not an agent
- **Don't overscope** - One clear purpose beats ten vague ones
- **Don't skip boundaries** - This is equally important as capabilities
- **Don't copy examples** - Create your own based on your work
