💡 Study Tips for AI Frontend Engineers
1
Start with AI-900 for Quick Win
Beginner-level, passable in 2-4 weeks, helps understand how AI integrates into user interfaces. This early win builds confidence and gives you the vocabulary to talk about AI capabilities with your team.
2
Design AI-First Experiences
The Google UX cert teaches design thinking. Apply it specifically to AI interfaces: chatbots, dashboards, confidence indicators. Understanding how users interact with unpredictable, generative outputs is what separates an AI frontend engineer from a traditional one.
3
Build an AI UX Portfolio
Create case studies showing how you designed interfaces for AI features. This portfolio proves more than any certificate alone. Document your design decisions, user research findings, and how you handled AI-specific UX challenges like latency, hallucination indicators, and progressive disclosure.
📅 4-Week Quick Start Study Plan
Week 1 — AI-900 Foundations
Azure AI Services & Frontend Impact
  • Start the AI-900 learning path on Microsoft Learn
  • Understand Azure AI services: Computer Vision, Language, Speech, Decision
  • Learn how AI capabilities impact frontend design decisions
  • Complete practice assessments for AI-900
Week 2 — AI-900 Exam + UX Start
Pass AI-900, Begin Google UX Design
  • Final review and pass the AI-900 certification exam
  • Begin Google UX Design Course 1 (Foundations of User Experience)
  • Learn UX design vocabulary: empathy maps, user stories, personas
  • Study Laws of UX principles and how they apply to AI interfaces
Week 3 — UX Design Deep Dive
Research, Wireframing & Prototyping
  • Learn user research methods: interviews, surveys, usability testing
  • Practice wireframing AI interfaces (chatbots, dashboards, recommendation UIs)
  • Start prototyping with Figma, apply design thinking process
  • Study how to design for AI-specific challenges: latency, confidence scores, error states
Week 4 — UX Portfolio
Case Studies & Portfolio Building
  • Build 2 case studies: one general UX, one AI-focused interface design
  • Create a portfolio piece demonstrating your design process end-to-end
  • Continue Google UX cert coursework (aim to complete Course 2)
  • Document design decisions and user research findings in your portfolio
